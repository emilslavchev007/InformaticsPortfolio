Chicken Alfredo with Fettuccine

  • Time: 25 min
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
Fettucine 12 ounces
Boneless, skinless, chicken breasts 12 ounces(about 2 breasts)
Unsalted butter 1 stick(8 table spoons)
Heavy cream 2 cups
Grated nutmeg 2 pinches
Freshy grated Parmigiano Reggiano cheese 1 1/2 cups
Kosher salt
Olive oil
Freshy ground black pepper

Step 1: Bring a large pot of water to a boil, add salt generously. Add the pasta and cook according to package directions until al dente. Strain and toss with a splash of olive oil.

Step 2: While water is boiling, slice the chicken into 1/4 inch thick. Season with salt and pepper.

Step 3: Heat a large skillet over medium heat. Add 2 tablespoons of the butter. When it melts, raise the heat to medium-high and add the chicken. Cook, without moving the chicken, until one side is browned. Flip the chicken and cook until thoroughly cooked. Transfer the chicken to a bowl when done.

Step 4: Change the heat to medium, adding the remainder of the butter. Scrape accross the bottom of the skillet with a wooden spoon to release any browned bits. When the butter is mostly melted, whisk in the cream and nutmeg. Bring it all to a simmer then cook for 2 more minutes. Lower the heat to keep the sauce warm.

Step 5: Whisk in the Parmigiano Reggiano cheese. Add the chicken and cooked pasta, toss thoroughly. Serve hot.

Source: Chicken Fettuccine Recipe by Food Network Kitchen

Avocado Milkshake

  • Time: 5 min
  • Serves: 1
Ingredients Quantity
2% Low fat milk 1 cup
Avocado 1
Sugar 2 tablespoons
Vanilla extract 1 teaspoon
Ice 1 cup

Step 1: Mix everything into a blender until it is smooth.

Optional: If you want a thicker mix, add more Ice. If you desire more vanilla flavour, add more vanilla extract. More sugar if you want it sweeter.

Source: Avocado Milkshake Recipe by WonderMima

Cream of Broccoli Soup

  • Time: 1 hour
  • Serves: 4-6
Ingredients Quantity
Butter 1 stick (8 tablespoons)
Flour 2/3 cups
Onion 1/4 cup
Broccoli florets cut into bite-sized pieces 4 cups
Chicken broth 6 tablespoons
Half-and-half (half cream half milk) 6 tablespoons
Salt 1 teaspoon
Pepper 1/4 teaspoon

Step 1: In a small saucepan, melt 6 tablespoons of butter over low heat. Add in flour and stir until blended. Do no brown.

Step 2: In a large saucepan, melt 2 tablespoons of butter. Add onion and cook at medium heat until tender. Add in the broccoli florets, and cook and stir for about 6 minutes.

Step 3: Add chicken broth, increase the heat and bring to a boil, whisking constantly.

Step 4: Add flour mixture, cook and stir until soup is thick and bubbly.

Step 5: Lastly, add salt, pepper, and half-and-half. Heat through and serve hot.

Note: The soup will thicken upon standing. It is possible to substitute cauliflower for broccoli. half-and-half can be substituted by a mixture of equal parts milk and cream.

Source: Cream of Broccoli Soup by Munchkin Mama
